## 1. The Anthropic Economic Index — Uneven Geographic and Enterprise AI Adoption
**Source:** Anthropic
**Date:** September 15, 2025
**Authors:** Ruth Appel, Peter McCrory, Alex Tamkin, Miles McCain, Tyler Neylon, Michael Stern
**File:** `reference/anthropic--economic-index--uneven-ai-adoption--sep-2025.pdf`
**Key insights:** AI adoption is highly uneven geographically and by firm size; 40% of US employees now report using AI at work (up from 20% in 2023); adoption concentrated in a small number of tasks and regions, mirroring historical technology diffusion patterns.

## 2. Canaries in the Coal Mine? Six Facts about the Recent Employment Effects of Artificial Intelligence
**Source:** Stanford University (Digital Economy Lab)
**Date:** November 13, 2025
**Authors:** Erik Brynjolfsson, Bharat Chandar, Ruyu Chen
**File:** `reference/stanford--canaries-in-the-coal-mine--employment-effects-of-ai--nov-2025.pdf`
**Key insights:** Early-career workers (ages 22–25) in AI-exposed occupations experienced 16% relative employment declines; experienced workers largely unaffected; AI automates rather than augments in affected roles; entry-level white-collar work is most at risk.

## 3. AI 2027
**Source:** AI Futures Project
**Date:** April 3, 2025
**Authors:** Daniel Kokotajlo, Scott Alexander, Thomas Larsen, Eli Lifland, Romeo Dean
**File:** `reference/ai-futures-project--ai-2027--apr-2025.pdf`
**Key insights:** Scenario-based forecast predicting superintelligence arrival by end of decade; projects AI impact exceeding the Industrial Revolution; provides detailed timeline from 2025 to 2027+ including economic disruption, job displacement, and societal change.

## 4. The State of AI in 2025 — Agents, Innovation, and Transformation
**Source:** McKinsey & Company (QuantumBlack)
**Date:** November 2025
**Authors:** Alex Singla, Alexander Sukharevsky, Bryce Hall, Lareina Yee, Michael Chui, Tara Balakrishnan
**File:** `reference/mckinsey--state-of-ai-in-2025--agents-innovation-transformation--nov-2025.pdf`
**Key insights:** Nearly all organizations are using AI but most remain in early stages; 62% experimenting with AI agents; 32% of organizations expect workforce reductions; high performers redesign workflows to capture AI value; enterprise transformation is underway but uneven.

## 5. The 7 Most Powerful Moats for AI Startups
**Source:** Y Combinator (The Light Cone Podcast)
**Speakers:** Gary Tan, Jared Friedman, Diana Hu, Harj Taggar
**File:** `reference/The 7 Most Powerful Moats for AI Startups.md`
**Key insights:** Speed is the primary early-stage moat. Process power (deep, painstaking engineering to get from 80% to 99% accuracy) creates defensibility that hackathon demos can't replicate. Counter-positioning against incumbents works because established companies can't cannibalize per-seat revenue. Data and evals create compounding network effects. Vertical AI SaaS can capture 10x the wallet share of traditional SaaS by automating work, not just organizing data.
